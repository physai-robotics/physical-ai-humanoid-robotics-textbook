## About This Course

This 13-week intensive course bridges the gap between Artificial Intelligence and Physical Robotics. You'll learn how to design, simulate, and deploy intelligent humanoid robots that can perceive, learn, and act in the real world.

### What You'll Learn

- **Foundations**: Robot kinematics, dynamics, and control theory
- **Perception**: Computer vision, sensor fusion, and embodied AI
- **Intelligence**: Machine learning, deep learning, and decision-making
- **Embodiment**: Humanoid design, bipedal locomotion, and physical interaction
- **Deployment**: Sim-to-real transfer, safety systems, and real-world deployment

## Course Structure

### 13-Week Curriculum

**Weeks 1-2**: Introduction to Physical AI
- What is Physical AI and why embodiment matters
- Overview of ROS 2, Gazebo, and NVIDIA Isaac

**Weeks 3-5**: ROS 2 Fundamentals
- Robot Operating System 2 architecture and best practices
- Publishing, subscribing, and real-time communication

**Weeks 6-7**: Robot Simulation with Gazebo
- Physics-based simulation with realistic dynamics
- URDF format and robot description

**Weeks 8-10**: NVIDIA Isaac Platform
- Digital twins and simulation-to-reality transfer
- Training AI models in simulation

**Weeks 11-12**: Humanoid Robot Development
- Bipedal locomotion and gait generation
- Motion planning and control

**Week 13**: Conversational Robotics
- Voice interaction and natural language processing
- Integration with AI agents

### Prerequisites

- Basic programming knowledge (Python/C++)
- Understanding of linear algebra and calculus
- Familiarity with machine learning concepts
